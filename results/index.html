Alexander Duan wins the 2026 National Geography Bee!

The National Geography Bee is pleased to announce the results of its first annual National Geography Bee! The top 38 students, from a pool of approximately 6,000 from around the country, competed in the national championships held at the Sheraton Commander in Cambridge, MA.

Alexander Duan, a 10th grader from Lexington, Massachusetts, prevailed in the championship round, correctly identifying Shandong as the Chinese province, home to both Mount Tai and the birthplace of Confucius, where the Yellow River empties into the Bohai Sea. Adam Brookens, an 11th grader from Bellefonte, Pennsylvania, came 2nd, and Gabriel Cabio, a 10th grader from San Antonio, Texas, came 3rd.

The National Geography Bee, Inc. is a registered 501(c)3 nonprofit organization dedicated to celebrating and inspiring curiosity in young people around the country through friendly and educational competition. Interested schools, sponsors, and partners can reach out at info@nationalgeobee.org. Registration for the 2026-2027 competition will open in the late summer/early fall of 2026.
